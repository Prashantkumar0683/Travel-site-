Whitehaven Beach

A World Heritage-listed paradise with pure white sands and crystal-clear waters.

Introduction to Whitehaven Beach

Whitehaven Beach, located on Whitsunday Island in Queensland, Australia, is one of the most famous and stunning beaches in the world. Known for its powdery white sand and turquoise waters, it is part of the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park. Whether you’re here to relax, explore, or take in breathtaking views, Whitehaven offers an unforgettable tropical experience.

Things to Do at Whitehaven Beach

Snorkeling

Explore vibrant coral reefs and encounter diverse marine life while snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters.

Hiking

Hike to Hill Inlet Lookout for stunning panoramic views of the swirling sands and turquoise waters of Whitehaven Beach.

Sailing

Take a sailing tour around the Whitsunday Islands and enjoy spectacular views of Whitehaven Beach from the water.

How to Get to Whitehaven Beach

Whitehaven Beach is easily accessible from Airlie Beach or Hamilton Island. Here are your best options:

By Boat

Regular boat tours depart from Airlie Beach, which is around 20 km from Whitehaven. These tours often combine snorkeling and sightseeing for a full day trip.

By Helicopter

For an aerial view of Whitehaven, consider booking a scenic helicopter ride from Airlie Beach or Hamilton Island. The bird’s-eye view of the swirling sands is spectacular.

Best Time to Visit Whitehaven Beach

The best time to visit Whitehaven Beach is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The weather is sunny, with warm temperatures and minimal rainfall, making it perfect for outdoor activities like snorkeling, hiking, and swimming.

However, if you prefer fewer crowds, consider visiting during the shoulder months of April or November.
